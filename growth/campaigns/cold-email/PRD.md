# Cold Email Campaign PRD
**Status:** Planning
**Goal:** Fill sales pipeline with qualified leads
**Target Scale:** 100-300 emails/day
**Timeline:** 4-6 weeks to launch

---

## Success Metrics

**Primary KPIs:**
- Reply rate: 2-5%
- Positive reply rate: 1-2%
- Meeting booking rate: 0.5-1%
- Meeting-to-close rate: 20-30%

**Minimum Viable Success:** 3 qualified meetings booked per week

---

## Phase 1: Technical Setup (Week 1-2)

### Domain Infrastructure
- [ ] Purchase 4-6 domains for outreach (~$60)
- [ ] Set up DNS records (SPF, DKIM, DMARC) for each
- [ ] Forward domains to main website (loxodonta.se)
- [ ] Set up custom tracking domain (track.satori-domain.com)

### Email Accounts
- [ ] Create 2-3 accounts per domain (12-18 total accounts)
- [ ] Use Google Workspace ($4/account/month = ~$60/month)
- [ ] Add profile photos and professional signatures
- [ ] Begin 14-day warmup process
  - Day 1-7: 10 emails/account/day
  - Day 8-14: Increase by 10% daily
  - Day 15+: Max 25 emails/account/day

**Budget:** ~$120 setup + $60/month ongoing

---

## Phase 2: Targeting (Week 2-3)

### Ideal Customer Profile (Swedish Market)
- **Primary:** Swedish organizations 50-500 employees
- **Verticals:** Professional associations, sports federations, cultural organizations
- **Decision makers:** General Secretary, CEO, Operations Manager
- **Pain points:** Manual processes, need for digital transformation, AI readiness

### Data Sources
- [ ] Apollo.io (Swedish companies + contacts)
- [ ] LinkedIn Sales Navigator (decision maker verification)
- [ ] Existing client research (find similar organizations)

### List Building Process
1. Export 3,000-5,000 contacts across 3-5 segments
2. Validate emails (MillionVerifier or VerifyEmailAI)
3. Expected valid rate: ~70% = 2,100-3,500 good emails

**Budget:** ~$30-50 for email validation

---

## Phase 3: Messaging (Week 3-4)

### Segment Strategy
Create 3-5 targeted segments:
1. **Sports federations** (ref: Svenska Volleybollförbundet success)
2. **Study associations** (ref: Folkbildningsrådet, Bilda)
3. **Professional associations** (general business orgs)
4. **Cultural organizations** (museums, arts councils)
5. **SMB service companies** (consulting, professional services)

### Email Template Framework

**Structure (4 parts):**
1. **Personal reason:** Why them specifically
2. **Value prop:** What we do + proof
3. **Simple CTA:** 15-min call or quick question
4. **Social proof:** Specific client result

**Technical specs:**
- Plain text only
- 50-100 words max
- Subject line: 6 words or less
- Use spintax for greetings/sign-offs
- No images, no fancy formatting

### Testing Protocol
- [ ] Test spam score (mail-tester.com)
- [ ] Send 50-100 test batch per template
- [ ] Monitor deliverability and reply rate
- [ ] Iterate based on results

---

## Phase 4: Launch & Optimize (Week 4+)

### Week 1: Initial Launch
- Send 100 emails/day (4 domains × 2 accounts × 12-13 emails)
- Monitor: deliverability, reply rate, spam complaints
- Adjust: timing, messaging, segments

### Week 2-3: Scale Up
- Increase to 200 emails/day if metrics are good
- Add 3rd email account per domain if needed
- Test follow-up sequences (2-4 follow-ups, 3-7 days apart)

### Week 4+: Optimize & Scale
- Reach target 300 emails/day
- A/B test: subject lines, CTAs, value props
- Double down on best-performing segments
- Refresh lists monthly

### Optimization Cycle (Weekly)
1. Review metrics (reply rate, meeting bookings)
2. Identify top/bottom performing segments
3. Refine messaging for top segments
4. Pause or replace bottom segments
5. Test 1-2 new variants

## Risk Mitigation

**Risk:** Low reply rates or poor deliverability
- **Mitigation:** Small test batches, gradual scale-up, proper warmup

**Risk:** Spam complaints hurt domain reputation
- **Mitigation:** Use separate domains, monitor feedback loops, clean opt-outs

**Risk:** Wrong target segments
- **Mitigation:** Test 3-5 segments simultaneously, pivot quickly based on data

**Risk:** Time-intensive reply management
- **Mitigation:** Set clear qualification criteria, use templates for common responses
